Transfer Mechanics — Comparative Scoring
Critical Findings — Tax & Jurisdiction
Malta Exit Tax 35% vs 5%
Whether the 6/7ths shareholder refund mechanism applies to exit taxation determines whether exit tax is ~5% (manageable) or 35% (potentially program-altering). Malta tax counsel must confirm before any transfer mechanic cost comparison.
URGENT
US CFC/GILTI Exposure
US LLC holding UAE entity triggers CFC rules. Subpart F, GILTI, and Section 1248 recharacterization must be analyzed. Delaware stays because GILTI at corporate level is ~2x more favorable than at UBO personal level.
URGENT
DIFC 9% vs Dutch 10% Test
Dutch participation exemption requires at least 10% subject-to-tax. DIFC at 9% may fail this threshold, fundamentally undermining the Netherlands option for travel holding. Dutch counsel must verify.
HIGH
Free Zone Misconception Resolved
DIFC and ADGM both operate under the same federal CT Law. Dividends and capital gains are exempt at federal level through participation exemption regardless of free zone selection. DIFC is the presumptive choice.
RESOLVED
Pillar Two Risk (EUR 750M)
If consolidated group revenue exceeds EUR 750M, Pillar Two global minimum tax at 15% could eliminate any free zone benefit. CFO must confirm the revenue threshold position.
HIGH
Sequence: NL First if Selected
If Netherlands selected for travel, optimal sequence: move travel first (Malta to NL under EU Merger Directive with exit tax deferral potential), then group holding (Malta to UAE). Reverse forecloses EU option.
HIGH
